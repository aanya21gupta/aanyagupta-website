My research sits at the intersection of computational immunology, machine learning, and data science. I have worked at Stanford's Maecker Lab since 10th grade, and have collaborated with the La Jolla Institute for Immunology and AIIMS/IIT-Delhi. My work focuses on building models to understand how biological systems — immune responses, aging, vaccine efficacy — behave at scale.

Comprehensive Analysis of Multi-Omics Vaccine Response Data Using MOFA and Stabl Algorithms
Frontiers in Bioinformatics · Stanford Maecker Lab
Statistical and ML-based analysis of the FluPRINT dataset. Researched various algorithms to address problems of large, complex datasets — creating models and tuning them through hyperparameter optimization. Explored the cellular and molecular underpinnings of successful antibody responses to flu vaccines using MoFA and Stabl algorithms.

First Author
Serum Cytokines During Acute Respiratory Infection and Relationship to Age
Journal of Cellular Immunology · Stanford Maecker Lab
Investigated the relation between influenza and aging using statistical analysis, including linear regression and Mann-Whitney tests, of cytokine levels in the FluPRINT dataset. Explored how biological age modulates the inflammatory landscape during acute respiratory infection.

Nature Communications
Submitted 2025
Co-Author
A Community Effort to Address Reproducibility Issues in Post-Vaccination Response Prediction
Nature Communications (submitted) · CMI-PB Challenge · La Jolla Institute for Immunology
Participated in this global competition aimed at building the best model for predicting pertussis vaccine response. Placed 1st in the T-cell Response Task — a competition open to university-level research teams worldwide. Effectively developed different methods including k-fold cross-validation and hyperparameter optimization of MoFA and Stabl models. Obtained 98% confidence interval of top correlation in cell frequency tasks. Presented at FOCIS Annual Meeting 2025.

Submitted 2025
Co-Author
Characterization of the Clivus (Foveal-Retinal) Angle Using Retinal Imaging and Machine Learning
Ophthalmology Retina (submitted) · AIIMS / IIT-Delhi · EURETINA Congress 2025
Developed a semi-automated AI tool and evaluated other machine learning-based methods for predicting and determining possible effects of biological traits on foveal angle measurement. Created an age-based database for foveal angle in the Indian population. Mentored by Dr. Abhinav Kaushik (Harvard University) and Dr. Pradeep Venkatesh (AIIMS, New Delhi).
